Statewide

Initiative 1351: K-12 education

This is about reducing class sizes in K-12 schools. Washington has some of the largest class sizes in the country.

What a Yes vote does

Class-size limits would take effect:

• Grades K-3: no more than 15 to 17 students.
• Grades 4-12: no more than 22 to 25 students.

Schools would need more teachers. The measure does not create a funding source, so lawmakers would need to find a way to pay for the hiring.

What a No vote does

Classroom sizes would not be subject to the proposed limits.

For comparison: the average elementary-school classroom in Washington has 23.7 students. For upper grades, the number is 29.7.

Initiative 107 and Proposed Alternative: Preschool

This is actually a twofer, with competing proposals. You can choose neither, or pick one, but you can’t approve both.

Initiative 107

This idea came from two unions. It would make rules for the price of preschool, and for how much preschool teachers get paid.
