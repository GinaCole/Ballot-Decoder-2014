Seattle measures

Initiative 107 and Proposed Alternative: Preschool

This is actually a twofer, with competing proposals. You can choose neither, or pick one, but you can’t approve both.

The first option: Initiative 107. This idea came from two unions. It would make rules for the price of preschool, and for how much preschool teachers get paid.

The second option: A property-tax levy. This is the city’s idea, and it's narrower than I-107. Preschools could opt in to receive the tax money if teachers meet certain criteria. For someone who owns a $400,000 home, it would cost about $43 a year.

The unions didn’t want their measure to compete with the property-tax levy, but the City Council put the two measures on the ballot that way anyway.
